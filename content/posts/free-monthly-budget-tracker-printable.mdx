## How to use it without overcomplicating things

Fill in your income and your three category targets at the start of the month, before any spending happens. This is the step people skip, and it is the one that actually makes a tracker work instead of becoming a record of regret after the fact.

Update it once a week, not once a day. Daily tracking is where most trackers get abandoned, because it turns budgeting into a chore instead of a five minute habit.

At the end of the month, do not just file it away. Circle whichever category went over target and ask what happened. That one honest answer is worth more than the tracker itself.

## Why paper still works

A budget stored inside an app is easy to forget about, because it is one swipe away from being closed and out of sight. A page taped somewhere you already look every day does not have that problem. It is not about being anti-technology. It is about picking the format you will actually use.

Print a few months at once if you want to stay ahead, or reprint one page each month as you go. Either way, the goal is the same: a plan you can see, not just one that exists somewhere on your phone.
